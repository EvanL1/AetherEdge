# Aether Community Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Aether a harassment-free experience for
everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We will act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness;
- respecting differing opinions, experiences, and technical backgrounds;
- giving and accepting constructive, specific feedback;
- taking responsibility, apologizing when appropriate, and learning from the
  experience;
- focusing on what is best for users, operators, contributors, and the wider
  community;
- treating safety concerns about physical devices and critical operations
  with care rather than dismissiveness.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- sexualized language or imagery, unwanted sexual attention, or advances;
- trolling, insults, derogatory comments, or personal and political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit
  permission;
- pressuring someone to disclose employer, customer, site, device, credential,
  or vulnerability information;
- retaliation against a good-faith conduct or security report;
- other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Scope

This Code applies in project spaces and when an individual officially
represents Aether in public spaces. Project spaces include repositories,
issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, release channels, and community
events operated by the project.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Repository maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code.
They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, reviews, and
other contributions that violate it, and will explain moderation decisions
when doing so is safe and appropriate.

## Reporting

For platform abuse, use GitHub's built-in abuse-reporting tools. For a project
conduct incident, contact a current repository maintainer privately using a
contact method published on that maintainer's GitHub profile. If no private
method is available, open an issue containing only a request for confidential
contact; do not disclose names, evidence, vulnerability details, or other
sensitive facts in that issue.

Security-sensitive reports must follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). Reports
will be considered by maintainers who are not involved in the incident when
that is possible. Maintainers will respect the reporter's privacy and safety
to the extent they can, but this project does not promise a fixed response
SLA or absolute confidentiality outside systems it controls.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will consider context, impact, intent, history, and safety when
selecting a response. Possible actions include:

1. **Correction** — a private or public explanation of the violation and a
   request to correct the behavior.
2. **Warning** — a warning with conditions on continued participation.
3. **Temporary restriction** — temporary loss of interaction, review, or
   contribution privileges.
4. **Permanent restriction** — permanent removal from project spaces for a
   pattern of violations or a serious incident.

Good-faith disagreement about architecture or review feedback is not, by
itself, a conduct violation. Critique the work and its consequences, not the
person.
